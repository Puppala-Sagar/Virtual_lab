Welcome to KMIT's Engineering Virtual Lab

Welcome to the Engineering Physics Virtual Lab – your gateway to a world of hands-on learning and experimentation in the realm of engineering physics. We are thrilled to present a revolutionary online platform that empowers students, educators, and enthusiasts to enhance one’s practical skills and knowledge through immersive simulations of our experiments.

Our virtual lab offers you the opportunity to explore intricate concepts, simulation, and theories of different experiments in a risk-free, digital environment. This website has been made for both teachers and students to provide a seamless way to not just learn but facilitate teaching as well.

Optimal Viewing Experience: For a seamless and enjoyable learning experience, we recommend utilizing this virtual lab on a laptop with a screen size of around 15.6 inches. This configuration ensures a hassle-free interaction with the simulations, allowing you to explore concepts with clarity and ease.

Important Considerations: It's important to recognize that this virtual lab is designed to enhance your learning journey, but it SHOULD NOT serve as your sole source of understanding. We STRONGLY ADVISE against relying solely on this platform. Instead, view it as an educational tool that complements your studies.

Precautionary: To derive the maximum benefit from this resource, we encourage you to integrate it into your educational approach. Consider using this platform either BEFORE or AFTER conducting the actual experiments. This strategic utilization will aid in deepening your grasp of the underlying principles.

Dynamic Development: Please be aware that this website is an ongoing project, and our team is continuously working to expand its offerings. More experiments will be added over time to enrich your learning experience.

Your Feedback Matters: In the event that you encounter any issues while using the platform, we value your feedback. Don't hesitate to reach out to your teachers or contact us directly to report any problems you come across. We are committed to resolving these issues promptly to ensure a smooth and productive experience for all users!

Overview

Each experiment in this virtual lab consists of three parts: Theory, Simulation, Self-Evaluation. You can find all three parts as a dropdown under each experiment in the navigation bar above

Theory: This sub-page comprises the underlying theory for each experiment, essential to grasp before conducting them. The theory to be documented in record remains consistent as well.

Simulation: The simulation sub-page provides students with the opportunity to engage in the experiment virtually.

Self-Evaluation: This sub-page has a Google form to test your knowledge regarding the experiment i.e. it is a VIVA form. Upon clicking submit the student can immediately check their score and reflect on their performance.
